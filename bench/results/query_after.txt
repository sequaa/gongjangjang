  INTERVIEW TALKING POINT: Partitioning is not free.
  For ALL three patterns, flat+index beats or ties part+index. The dominant gain
  in all patterns is the index (IDX_R), NOT partitioning (PART_R = 0.7-0.8x).
  Partitioning helps only when the partition key aligns with a WHERE predicate
  AND the table is large enough that pruning removes more work than Append adds.
  At 5M rows with a good B-tree index, the index alone is sufficient — partitioning
  adds overhead without benefit for these query shapes. Knowing this distinction
  is the differentiating insight — not just 'partitioning = faster'.
